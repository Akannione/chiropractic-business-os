# Internal Sales Notes

## Positioning

The Chiropractic Business OS is a lightweight visibility dashboard for patient inquiries and follow-ups. It is not an EHR, scheduler, billing system, or patient portal.

## Buyer

- Chiropractic practice owner
- Office manager
- Front-desk lead responsible for patient follow-up

## Core Pain

Patient inquiries arrive from multiple places, but follow-up tracking is scattered. This creates missed calls, stale inquiries, and limited visibility into estimated treatment value.

## Sales Angle

Help the practice owner see:

- Which patient inquiries need follow-up
- Which follow-ups are overdue
- Which inquiries became active patients
- What estimated treatment value is still in the pipeline
- What happened this week

## Pricing

- Starter Demo Setup: $500
- Practice Launch: $1,500
- Workflow Polish: $3,000

## Strong Discovery Questions

- How do you know whether every inquiry was followed up?
- What happens when someone asks about insurance but does not book?
- Do you know which inquiry source produces the most active patients?
- Would a weekly practice snapshot help you manage the front desk?

## Objection Tracking Prompts

- Did they say they already have an EHR, scheduler, CRM, or spreadsheet?
- Did they worry about HIPAA, data privacy, or patient information?
- Did they question whether inquiry follow-up is a big enough problem?
- Did they hesitate on price or timing?
- Did they ask for features outside the current scope?
- Which response created the most trust?
- Should this objection change the offer copy, demo script, or FAQ?
- Should this objection be captured in `sales_assets/objection_handling.md` for future calls?

## Common Objections

### We already have an EHR.

This does not replace the EHR. It focuses on inquiry and follow-up visibility before or around the booking workflow.

### We use spreadsheets.

This keeps spreadsheet simplicity while adding KPI visibility, weekly summaries, and cleaner exports.

### Is this HIPAA compliant?

The current demo uses fake data. Production use would need proper hosting, policies, access controls, and compliance review.

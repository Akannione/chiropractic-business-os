# Narrated Demo Video Script

This script is used for the narrated `sales_assets/demo_video.mp4`. Keep it focused on the benefits of the existing demo tabs and avoid promising features that are not in the app.

## Narration

Welcome to the Chiropractic Business OS demo. This is a simple practice visibility tool for chiropractors who want a clearer way to track patient inquiries, follow-ups, and estimated treatment value without replacing their EHR, scheduler, or billing system.

The goal is straightforward: help the practice owner and front desk see what needs attention today. Many practices receive inquiries from Google, referrals, insurance questions, the website, and phone calls. When those conversations live in different places, it becomes easy to miss a follow-up or lose track of which inquiries became active patients.

We start on the Dashboard. This screen is designed to be understood quickly. Total Patient Inquiries shows the full inquiry list. New This Week shows recent activity since Monday. Active Patients shows inquiries that turned into patients. Follow-Ups Needed highlights anyone marked for follow-up or due today. Overdue Follow-Ups makes delayed outreach visible before it becomes a lost opportunity. Estimated Treatment Value shows the total potential treatment revenue from inquiries that have not been marked lost. The conversion rate shows what percentage of inquiries became active patients, and Top Inquiry Source shows where the most inquiries are coming from.

Next is the Patient Inquiries workspace. This is where a team member can add a new inquiry, capture the requested service, record the source, estimate treatment value, and schedule the next follow-up. The language is intentionally practice-friendly: requested services include examples like spinal adjustment, sports injury treatment, wellness consultation, neck pain evaluation, and lower back pain care. Statuses are also simple: New Inquiry, Consultation Scheduled, Active Patient, Lost, and Follow-Up Needed.

The inquiry details section keeps follow-up work close to each patient inquiry. Instead of forcing the office to manage a separate tracker, the team can review notes, change status, and set the next follow-up date in one place. Overdue and due-today follow-ups are emphasized so the front desk knows where to focus first.

The Weekly Summary tab gives the owner a plain-English practice performance snapshot. It summarizes total inquiries, new inquiries this week, active patients, follow-ups needed, overdue follow-ups, estimated treatment value, conversion rate, and top inquiry source. This makes it easier to review the week without digging through spreadsheets or guessing which follow-ups are slipping.

The Export tab is for simple handoff and review. The app provides patient-friendly CSV exports for inquiries and follow-up queues. The export labels are written for a practice owner, not a developer, so the files are easier to understand during a demo or internal review.

This demo uses fake sample data only. It is not a HIPAA production system yet, and it does not add scheduling, payments, or EHR features. The value of this version is focus: it helps a chiropractic practice see inquiry activity, follow-up urgency, and potential treatment revenue in one simple place.

The best question after the demo is: would this help your practice avoid missed follow-ups and understand which inquiries are turning into active patients?

# Discovery Call Playbook

Purpose: keep chiropractic discovery calls consultative, consistent, qualification-focused, and non-salesy.

## 1. Opening

Simple introduction:

```text
Thanks for taking the time. I want to understand how your practice currently handles new patient inquiries and follow-ups. If there is a fit, I can show how the Chiropractic Business OS works. If there is not a fit, I will say that clearly.
```

Agenda:

1. Understand current inquiry workflow.
2. Learn how follow-ups are handled.
3. Review reporting and owner visibility.
4. Identify whether the Business OS is a fit.
5. Agree on the next step.

## 2. Rapport Building

Use light, practice-focused conversation starters:

- How long has the practice been serving the area?
- What types of patients do you see most often?
- Are most new patients coming from Google, referrals, insurance, or phone calls?
- Has inquiry volume changed recently?
- What keeps the front desk busiest during the week?

Keep rapport short. The goal is trust, not small talk for its own sake.

## 3. Current Process Questions

- How do new patient inquiries usually come in?
- Who manages them?
- How are they tracked today?
- What happens after someone asks a question but does not book immediately?
- Where do notes about inquiries live?
- Can the owner or office manager quickly see all open inquiries?

## 4. Inquiry Tracking Questions

- About how many patient inquiries do you receive each month?
- What are the most common inquiry sources?
- How are phone inquiries recorded?
- How are website inquiries recorded?
- Do referrals get tracked separately?
- Do you track requested services or reasons for inquiry?

## 5. Follow-Up Questions

- Who follows up with patient inquiries?
- How quickly does follow-up usually happen?
- What happens if someone does not respond?
- How do you know which follow-ups are due today?
- How do you know which follow-ups are overdue?
- What causes follow-ups to fall through the cracks?

## 6. Reporting Questions

- What numbers do you review weekly?
- How do you know if follow-up is working?
- Do you know how many inquiries become active patients?
- Do you know which inquiry source brings the most opportunities?
- Do you review estimated treatment value from open inquiries?
- What does the owner wish they could see faster?

## 7. Pain Point Questions

- What is most frustrating about the current process?
- Where do inquiries typically fall through the cracks?
- What takes too much manual effort?
- What does the front desk avoid updating?
- What would make weekly review easier?
- What would reduce uncertainty for the owner?

## 8. Budget Questions

- Have you invested in operational tools before?
- How do you usually evaluate these types of improvements?
- Do you prefer a small pilot before a larger rollout?
- What would make the setup feel worth the time and cost?
- Who needs to approve the purchase?

Keep this section calm. Do not pressure the prospect.

## 9. Closing Questions

- Based on what we discussed, would it make sense to show you how the system works?
- What would need to happen for a solution like this to be valuable?
- Is there anything that would make this a poor fit?
- Who else should be part of the demo or decision?
- What is the best next step?

## 10. Qualification Checkpoints

Map answers back to the qualification scorecard:

- Practice Size: solo, small team, multi-provider, or enterprise-style practice.
- Inquiry Volume: monthly inquiry volume and number of channels.
- Tracking Method: spreadsheet, notes, inbox, EHR, scheduler, CRM, or memory.
- Follow-Up Process: ownership, speed, overdue tracking, and consistency.
- Reporting Maturity: weekly owner view, source tracking, and active patient tracking.
- Technology Openness: willingness to pilot a simple dashboard without replacing existing systems.

## Recommended Next Steps

Choose one:

- No fit: thank them and stop active follow-up.
- Needs more information: send demo website, ROI calculator, or one-page offer.
- Demo fit: schedule a live walkthrough.
- Proposal fit: send proposal after demo and confirmed package.
- Timing issue: set a specific follow-up date.

Do not send a proposal if the practice is not at least a Possible Fit.

# Trust Audit

Purpose: track the trust and conversion improvements made before June 15 outreach.

## Findings

- The demo website headline described the product but did not clearly say why a practice owner should request a conversation.
- The problem statement was accurate but could be more direct about scattered inquiries, missed follow-ups, and lack of an owner view.
- Customer-facing materials used some generic terms such as `KPI Dashboard`, `visibility`, and `conversion rate` that may feel technical to a non-technical practice owner.
- The materials needed clearer boundaries around what the system is not: EHR, billing software, insurance software, or scheduling software.
- The offer materials needed a simple fit profile so prospects can self-qualify before a walkthrough.
- ROI language needed to stay conservative and avoid guaranteed outcomes.

## Changes Made

- Updated the demo website headline and hero copy to focus on seeing patient inquiries, follow-ups, and estimated treatment value in one place.
- Strengthened the problem statement around scattered inquiry tracking and inconsistent follow-up.
- Added clearer call-to-action placement on the hero, solution section, trust section, and contact section.
- Added `Who This Is For`, `Who This Is Not For`, and `What This Does Not Do` sections to the demo website.
- Added a conservative ROI example that avoids guarantees.
- Added `Why Practices Choose This` trust language focused on simplicity, seeing inquiries in one place, follow-up consistency, and faster onboarding.
- Updated FAQ wording to clarify EHR, scheduling, HIPAA, and spreadsheet boundaries.
- Updated editable marketing materials with consistent `Who This Is For`, `What This Is Not`, onboarding timeline, and support sections.
- Updated sales asset documentation and narration language to use `Practice Performance Dashboard` and `Inquiry-to-Patient Rate`.

## Future Trust Improvements

- Replace placeholder contact email with a real outreach inbox or booking link once ready.
- Add real client testimonials after the first successful pilot.
- Add a short case study once a practice has used the workflow long enough to report results.
- Add approved screenshots from a real client deployment with private information removed.
- Add a brief privacy and deployment explanation for production pilots.
- Add a one-page handoff summary for practice owners after live demos.

## Proof Assets Needed After First Client

- Written testimonial from the owner or office manager.
- Before-and-after follow-up workflow notes.
- Approved quote about reduced missed opportunities or improved follow-up consistency.
- Screenshot of a populated dashboard using anonymized or approved data.
- Short case study covering problem, setup, workflow, outcome, and next steps.
- Client-approved FAQ additions based on real buying concerns.

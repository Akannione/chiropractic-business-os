# First 20 Prospect Strategy

Goal: identify the first 20 chiropractic practices most likely to become paying clients before June 15 outreach.

Use this strategy with `qualification_scorecard.md`, `targeting_framework.md`, and `chiropractic_prospect_tracker.xlsx`.

## Geographic Targeting Recommendations

Prioritize practices where local competition and online inquiry volume are likely visible:

- Metro suburbs with many family practices and wellness clinics.
- Mid-sized cities where practices compete for Google visibility.
- Areas with active local business directories and Google Business Profiles.
- Markets where chiropractic practices list online booking, contact forms, or multiple inquiry channels.

Avoid starting with:

- Very rural practices with unclear inquiry volume.
- Large enterprise healthcare groups.
- Practices with no website, no clear contact information, and no visible online activity.

## Solo vs Multi-Provider Guidance

### Solo Chiropractors

Best when:

- The owner is directly involved in follow-up.
- The practice uses spreadsheets, notes, or memory.
- Inquiry volume is enough to create missed-follow-up risk.

Watch for:

- Very low inquiry volume.
- Owner too busy to adopt even a simple workflow.

### Small Multi-Provider Practices

Best when:

- There is a front desk or office manager.
- Multiple inquiry sources create tracking friction.
- The owner wants weekly visibility without manually asking staff.

Watch for:

- More complex approval process.
- Existing systems that already solve the problem.

## Marketing Activity Indicators

Prioritize practices showing signs that new inquiries matter:

- Active Google Business Profile.
- Recent reviews.
- Website contact form.
- Phone number prominently displayed.
- Services pages for common chiropractic needs.
- Referral or insurance messaging.
- Social media activity within the last 60 days.

Higher marketing activity usually means higher inquiry volume and stronger fit.

## Website Quality Indicators

Positive indicators:

- Clear services page.
- Contact form or request appointment button.
- Multiple service categories.
- Recent content or updated design.
- Staff/provider page.
- Patient testimonials or reviews.

Negative indicators:

- Broken contact form.
- No clear phone number.
- No service detail.
- Website appears abandoned.
- Enterprise system language that suggests the buyer may not be local or accessible.

## Technology Adoption Indicators

Positive indicators:

- Uses online forms.
- Has active Google profile.
- Uses a website chat/contact widget.
- Mentions online appointment requests.
- Staff emails are listed.

Caution indicators:

- No visible digital tools.
- Website not updated.
- Only phone contact listed.
- No sign of review, inquiry, or front-desk workflow.

## First 20 Selection Rule

Build the first 20 list with this mix:

- 10 Tier 1 prospects.
- 7 Tier 2 prospects.
- 3 Tier 3 prospects with interesting signals.

Do not fill the first 20 with low-priority practices just to hit a number. A smaller, better-qualified list is more useful than a larger weak list.

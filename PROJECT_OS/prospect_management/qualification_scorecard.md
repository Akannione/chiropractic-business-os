# Chiropractic Practice Qualification Scorecard

Purpose: prioritize chiropractic practices most likely to become paying clients for the Chiropractic Business OS.

Use this before outreach when enough public information is available, then update the score after discovery calls. The total score is 1-100.

## Ideal Customer Profile

- Solo chiropractor or small chiropractic practice.
- Receives enough monthly patient inquiries that missed follow-up matters.
- Currently tracks inquiries in spreadsheets, notes, inboxes, or memory.
- Has inconsistent follow-up or limited owner visibility.
- Wants a simple practice dashboard, not a full EHR replacement.
- Open to a lightweight setup, training, and weekly review habit.

## Scoring System

### Practice Size: 15 Points

- 15 points: solo or small practice with 1-3 providers and a front desk or office manager.
- 10 points: growing practice with several providers but still simple operations.
- 5 points: very small practice with limited inquiry volume.
- 0 points: enterprise group, hospital network, or organization likely needing complex systems.

### Monthly Inquiry Volume: 20 Points

- 20 points: likely 20+ inquiries/month across phone, Google, website, referrals, and insurance questions.
- 15 points: likely 10-19 inquiries/month.
- 10 points: likely 5-9 inquiries/month.
- 5 points: fewer than 5 inquiries/month or unclear demand.
- 0 points: no meaningful new-patient inquiry flow.

### Current Tracking Method: 20 Points

- 20 points: spreadsheets, notebooks, inboxes, sticky notes, or memory.
- 15 points: spreadsheet plus inconsistent manual process.
- 10 points: EHR or scheduler used for some tracking, but inquiry-stage tracking is unclear.
- 5 points: mature CRM already used well.
- 0 points: robust process already solves inquiry tracking and follow-up.

### Follow-Up Process: 20 Points

- 20 points: follow-up ownership is unclear or frequently missed.
- 15 points: follow-up happens but is inconsistent.
- 10 points: follow-up is mostly consistent but hard for the owner to review.
- 5 points: follow-up is well-owned and reviewed regularly.
- 0 points: no follow-up pain or no interest in improving it.

### Reporting Maturity: 10 Points

- 10 points: owner lacks a weekly view of inquiries, active patients, sources, and follow-up pressure.
- 7 points: some reporting exists but requires manual spreadsheet work.
- 4 points: reporting exists in another tool but is not practice-owner friendly.
- 0 points: owner already has clear weekly reporting and uses it.

### Openness To Technology: 15 Points

- 15 points: open to simple tools and fast pilots.
- 10 points: cautious but willing to review a demo.
- 5 points: skeptical and needs strong proof.
- 0 points: strongly resistant to any new workflow.

## Qualification Thresholds

- 80-100: Ideal Prospect
- 65-79: Strong Prospect
- 50-64: Possible Fit
- Below 50: Low Priority

## Red Flags

- Wants an EHR replacement.
- Wants scheduling, billing, insurance, payment, or patient portal features.
- Requires enterprise permissions or multi-location complexity immediately.
- Refuses to assign a staff owner for follow-up updates.
- Has no inquiry volume or no follow-up problem.
- Expects guaranteed revenue.
- Wants to use real patient data before hosting, privacy, access, and compliance expectations are clear.

## Discovery Questions To Improve The Score

1. How many new patient inquiries do you receive in a typical month?
2. Where do inquiries come from: phone, Google, website, referrals, insurance, or other sources?
3. How are inquiries tracked today?
4. Who owns follow-up after someone asks a question but does not book?
5. How do you know which follow-ups are overdue?
6. Do you review weekly inquiry activity as an owner or manager?
7. What happens when a front-desk team member is out?
8. Do you already use a CRM, spreadsheet, EHR workflow, or scheduler for inquiry tracking?
9. Would a simple dashboard help, or are you looking for a larger system?
10. What would make a pilot feel worth the setup effort?

# Discovery Call Evaluation Scorecard

Use this scorecard after each rehearsal or real discovery call.

Scoring scale:

- 1: weak or missing.
- 2: attempted but unclear.
- 3: acceptable.
- 4: strong.
- 5: excellent and natural.

## Category Guidance

Rapport:

- Did the opening feel calm, relevant, and practice-focused?
- Did the conversation avoid sounding scripted or rushed?

Discovery quality:

- Were questions open-ended?
- Did the operator understand the current inquiry and follow-up workflow before pitching?

Qualification quality:

- Were practice size, inquiry volume, tracking method, follow-up process, reporting maturity, and technology openness explored?
- Was the qualification score improved after the call?

Objection handling:

- Were objections acknowledged without arguing?
- Did responses stay honest and non-guaranteed?

Positioning clarity:

- Was the system explained as inquiry tracking, follow-up visibility, weekly summary, and simple practice performance reporting?
- Were EHR, scheduling, billing, and insurance boundaries clear?

Closing effectiveness:

- Was the next step specific?
- Did the close match the prospect's level of interest?

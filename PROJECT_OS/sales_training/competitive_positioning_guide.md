# Competitive Positioning Guide

Use this guide during chiropractor outreach when prospects compare the Chiropractic Business OS against tools they already know.

## Positioning Summary

The Chiropractic Business OS is a lightweight inquiry and follow-up visibility tool. It is not an EHR, scheduler, billing system, CRM replacement, or patient portal. Its strongest position is helping a practice owner see missed follow-up risk, inquiry activity, estimated treatment value, and weekly practice performance in one simple place.

## Excel

### Advantages

- Familiar to most owners and office managers.
- Flexible and inexpensive.
- Easy to export or share internally.

### Disadvantages

- Follow-up urgency is easy to miss.
- Manual formatting and filtering can break.
- Weekly visibility depends on someone maintaining the spreadsheet.
- Harder to present as an owner-ready dashboard.

### Positioning

The Business OS keeps spreadsheet simplicity but adds a cleaner dashboard, follow-up visibility, weekly summary, and patient-friendly exports.

### Likely Objections

- "We already track this in Excel."
- "I do not want another tool."
- "Spreadsheets are enough for our office."

### Recommended Response

"If Excel is already working perfectly, this may not be necessary. The gap this solves is when follow-ups get buried, the owner cannot quickly see treatment value, or the front desk spends too much time filtering a sheet before knowing who needs attention."

## Google Sheets

### Advantages

- Easy team access.
- Cloud-based collaboration.
- Simple and familiar.
- Good for lightweight tracking.

### Disadvantages

- No guided workflow.
- Follow-up accountability depends on manual discipline.
- Metrics and charts can become messy.
- Owner-level weekly summaries often require extra setup.

### Positioning

The Business OS provides a focused front-desk workflow and practice-owner view while still allowing CSV exports when spreadsheet review is needed.

### Likely Objections

- "We already use Google Sheets."
- "Everyone can access our current sheet."
- "Why not just improve the sheet?"

### Recommended Response

"A better sheet may be enough for some practices. This is for when the practice wants a simple operating screen, not just a shared table. It makes follow-up risk, active patients, and estimated treatment value visible faster."

## HubSpot

### Advantages

- Mature CRM.
- Strong contact management.
- Email and pipeline tooling.
- Good for broader sales teams.

### Disadvantages

- Can feel too broad for a small chiropractic front desk.
- Requires setup and CRM discipline.
- Language is not chiropractic-specific by default.
- May be more tool than needed for inquiry follow-up.

### Positioning

The Business OS is narrower and simpler. It focuses on chiropractic inquiry intake, follow-up scheduling, estimated treatment value, and weekly practice visibility.

### Likely Objections

- "Can HubSpot already do this?"
- "Why not use a real CRM?"
- "We may need automation later."

### Recommended Response

"HubSpot is a strong CRM. This is intentionally smaller. If the practice needs a full CRM, HubSpot may be the better fit. If the current pain is missed inquiries and simple owner visibility, this is faster to understand and easier to pilot."

## Salesforce

### Advantages

- Enterprise-grade CRM.
- Highly customizable.
- Strong reporting and permissions.
- Scales across large teams.

### Disadvantages

- Heavy for a small practice.
- Expensive and time-consuming to configure.
- Requires strong administration.
- Not designed around a chiropractic front-desk workflow out of the box.

### Positioning

The Business OS is a small-business alternative for simple inquiry and follow-up visibility. It is not trying to compete with enterprise CRM depth.

### Likely Objections

- "Is this as powerful as Salesforce?"
- "What happens when we grow?"
- "Can it handle advanced workflows?"

### Recommended Response

"Salesforce is much more powerful. This is not trying to replace that. The value here is speed, clarity, and a focused workflow for a practice that wants to improve follow-up without taking on an enterprise CRM project."

## Chiropractic EHR Systems

### Advantages

- Built for clinical records.
- Often supports scheduling, billing, documentation, and patient history.
- Better fit for regulated production patient workflows.
- May already be part of the practice's daily operations.

### Disadvantages

- Inquiry follow-up before booking may be limited or hard to see.
- Owner-level pipeline visibility may not be obvious.
- Estimated treatment opportunity may not be tracked cleanly.
- Staff may avoid adding early inquiries before a formal patient record exists.

### Positioning

The Business OS does not replace the EHR. It sits around the inquiry and follow-up workflow where prospects can fall through the cracks before becoming active patients.

### Likely Objections

- "Our EHR already handles patients."
- "We cannot put patient data outside the EHR."
- "Is this HIPAA compliant?"

### Recommended Response

"The EHR should remain the clinical source of truth. This demo uses fake data and focuses on inquiry visibility. Any production use with real patient information would require a separate compliance, hosting, access-control, and policy plan."

## Outcome-Focused Talking Points

- Fewer missed patient inquiries.
- Faster follow-up for overdue and due-today inquiries.
- Clearer weekly visibility for the owner.
- Simpler setup than a broad CRM.
- Not a replacement for the EHR or scheduler.

# Common Sales Mistakes

Use this guide before rehearsals and live discovery calls.

## Talking Too Much

Why it happens:

- Nervousness.
- Trying to prove value too quickly.
- Filling silence instead of asking better questions.

Why it hurts:

- The practice owner feels pitched instead of understood.
- Important workflow details are missed.

Better approach:

- Ask one clear question, listen, summarize, then ask the next question.

## Pitching Too Early

Why it happens:

- The product feels ready and useful.
- The operator wants to show the demo before understanding the problem.

Why it hurts:

- The demo may feel generic.
- The prospect may not connect features to their actual workflow.

Better approach:

- Complete discovery first, then show only the parts that match the practice's pain.

## Feature Dumping

Why it happens:

- Trying to mention every tab, report, export, and asset.

Why it hurts:

- The prospect may feel overwhelmed.
- The most important value gets buried.

Better approach:

- Focus on three outcomes: track inquiries in one place, make follow-ups visible, review weekly performance.

## Arguing With Objections

Why it happens:

- Objections can feel like rejection.
- The operator wants to correct the prospect immediately.

Why it hurts:

- Trust drops.
- The prospect may stop sharing useful concerns.

Better approach:

- Acknowledge the concern, clarify the real issue, and answer honestly.

## Overpromising ROI

Why it happens:

- The ROI calculator can make the opportunity feel obvious.

Why it hurts:

- Revenue guarantees hurt trust.
- Results depend on practice behavior, inquiry volume, and follow-up consistency.

Better approach:

- Use conservative estimates and say clearly that results vary and are not guaranteed.

## Attacking Existing Software

Why it happens:

- Comparing against spreadsheets, EHRs, or CRMs can become too aggressive.

Why it hurts:

- The prospect may feel judged for their current process.
- The conversation can become defensive.

Better approach:

- Position the system as a simple operating layer for inquiry visibility, not a replacement for every tool.

# Chiropractic Objection Library

Use this during outreach practice and post-call review. The goal is honest positioning, not pressure.

## Cost

### 1. "This is too expensive."

- Why the owner is saying it: They do not yet connect the setup cost to missed follow-up risk or weekly owner visibility.
- Wrong response: "It will definitely make you more money."
- Better response: "That is fair. I would only position this as worthwhile if missed follow-ups are costing the practice attention or opportunities. We can use conservative numbers to estimate whether the setup makes sense."

### 2. "We cannot afford another software cost."

- Why the owner is saying it: They may be tired of subscriptions or tools that staff do not use.
- Wrong response: "This is cheaper than most software."
- Better response: "The goal is not to add another tool for its own sake. This should stay small: one place to review inquiries, follow-ups, and weekly practice activity."

### 3. "Can you do it for less?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They are testing price flexibility or unsure about value.
- Wrong response: "Sure, I can discount it."
- Better response: "We can reduce scope, but I would not want to cut the parts that make it useful: setup, training, and follow-up workflow handoff."

### 4. "Why pay for this when Google Sheets is free?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They see the app as a prettier spreadsheet.
- Wrong response: "Spreadsheets are bad."
- Better response: "A sheet can work. This is for practices that want a clearer owner dashboard, follow-up urgency, and weekly summary without manual filtering."

### 5. "What if we pay and staff do not use it?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They have seen software fail because habits did not change.
- Wrong response: "They will use it because it is easy."
- Better response: "Usage is the real risk. That is why rollout should include a narrow daily habit: add inquiries, update follow-up dates, and review follow-ups weekly."

## ROI

### 6. "How do I know this will pay for itself?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They need a business case, not feature talk.
- Wrong response: "It pays for itself if you get one patient."
- Better response: "We can estimate it, not guarantee it. Use monthly inquiries, missed follow-up rate, recovery rate, and average new patient value to see whether the risk is meaningful."

### 7. "Can you guarantee recovered revenue?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They want certainty before spending.
- Wrong response: "Yes, this will recover missed revenue."
- Better response: "No. Results vary by practice. The tool improves follow-up visibility, but staff still need to use it consistently."

### 8. "We do not miss follow-ups."

- Why the owner is saying it: They may trust the current front-desk process or feel criticized.
- Wrong response: "You probably do miss them."
- Better response: "If follow-up is already consistent, this may not be needed. The demo is useful only if it reveals a gap or saves review time."

### 9. "We do not get enough inquiries for this."

- Why the owner is saying it: Inquiry volume may be low or seasonal.
- Wrong response: "Every practice needs this."
- Better response: "Low volume may reduce the value. The better fit is a practice with enough inquiries that missed follow-up or owner visibility is becoming a real issue."

### 10. "The ROI calculator feels salesy."

- Why the owner is saying it: They distrust inflated numbers.
- Wrong response: "The calculator proves the ROI."
- Better response: "It is only a discussion estimate. We should use conservative inputs and treat it as a way to decide whether a pilot is worth discussing."

### 11. "Our average patient value is hard to estimate."

- Why the owner is saying it: Treatment value varies by patient and case type.
- Wrong response: "Just pick a high number."
- Better response: "Use a conservative average or a range. The point is not perfect accounting; it is understanding the possible cost of missed opportunities."

## Trust

### 12. "Why should I trust this?"

- Why the owner is saying it: The product is new and not a known vendor.
- Wrong response: "Because it works."
- Better response: "Start with the demo, fake data, screenshots, exports, and clear limitations. Trust should come from a narrow workflow and transparent boundaries."

### 13. "Is this HIPAA compliant?"

- Why the owner is saying it: Patient privacy is a serious concern.
- Wrong response: "Yes, it is fine."
- Better response: "The demo uses fake data. Production use with real patient data requires proper hosting, access controls, privacy policies, and compliance review."

### 14. "Where is the data stored?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They are worried about control and persistence.
- Wrong response: "Do not worry about it."
- Better response: "The MVP uses SQLite. For production, the database path and hosting persistence need to be planned before real data is used."

### 15. "What happens if the app breaks?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They worry about operational dependency.
- Wrong response: "It will not break."
- Better response: "The app is intentionally simple, and exports reduce lock-in. A paid rollout should include support expectations and a backup/export process."

### 16. "Is this professional enough for a practice?"

- Why the owner is saying it: Streamlit or MVP design may feel less polished than enterprise software.
- Wrong response: "It is just as good as enterprise software."
- Better response: "It is not enterprise software. It is a focused pilot dashboard designed to validate whether the workflow helps before investing in a heavier build."

### 17. "Are you going to use our data for demos?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They are concerned about confidentiality.
- Wrong response: "No one will notice."
- Better response: "No. Demo materials should use fake or explicitly approved anonymized data only."

## Existing Software

### 18. "We already have an EHR."

- Why the owner is saying it: They think this is trying to replace their clinical system.
- Wrong response: "Your EHR is not enough."
- Better response: "Your EHR should remain the clinical source of truth. This focuses on inquiry follow-up before or around booking."

### 19. "We already have scheduling software."

- Why the owner is saying it: They confuse inquiry tracking with appointment scheduling.
- Wrong response: "This scheduling workflow is better."
- Better response: "This does not schedule appointments. It helps the team see which inquiries need follow-up so the existing scheduler gets used with better context."

### 20. "We already use a spreadsheet."

- Why the owner is saying it: Their current process is familiar and cheap.
- Wrong response: "Spreadsheets are messy."
- Better response: "If the sheet is working, keep it. This helps when follow-up urgency, weekly summaries, or owner review require too much manual filtering."

### 21. "We use HubSpot or another CRM."

- Why the owner is saying it: They may already have a broad sales tool.
- Wrong response: "This is better than HubSpot."
- Better response: "A mature CRM may be the right tool. This is narrower and useful only if the practice wants a chiropractic-specific inquiry dashboard."

### 22. "Can it integrate with my EHR?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They want fewer duplicate workflows.
- Wrong response: "Yes, we can connect anything."
- Better response: "Not in this version. Integration should be scoped only after the inquiry workflow proves valuable."

### 23. "Can it send texts or emails automatically?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They want automation to remove manual follow-up.
- Wrong response: "Yes, automation is easy."
- Better response: "Not in this version. Messaging adds compliance and operational complexity. This version shows who needs follow-up."

## Workflow Disruption

### 24. "My front desk is already busy."

- Why the owner is saying it: They worry the tool creates extra work.
- Wrong response: "It only takes a minute."
- Better response: "That is the main workflow question. The tool should replace scattered tracking, not create duplicate entry."

### 25. "I do not want duplicate data entry."

- Why the owner is saying it: They fear maintaining both the EHR and this dashboard.
- Wrong response: "Duplicate entry is not a big deal."
- Better response: "The dashboard should only track the inquiry/follow-up stage where visibility is missing. It should not duplicate clinical records."

### 26. "Staff will forget to update it."

- Why the owner is saying it: They know software fails without ownership.
- Wrong response: "The system will remind them."
- Better response: "The current version does not automate reminders. Success depends on assigning one owner and reviewing follow-ups on a simple schedule."

### 27. "What changes in our daily routine?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They want to understand operational impact.
- Wrong response: "Nothing changes."
- Better response: "The smallest routine is: add new inquiries, set the next follow-up date, and review due or overdue follow-ups."

### 28. "Will this slow down phone calls?"

- Why the owner is saying it: Front-desk speed matters.
- Wrong response: "No, it is instant."
- Better response: "The intake should be used at the right point in the call workflow. If it slows calls down, the process needs adjustment."

## Time

### 29. "How long does setup take?"

- Why the owner is saying it: They are worried about disruption and hidden work.
- Wrong response: "It is quick, do not worry."
- Better response: "A demo setup is light. A production setup depends on data, hosting, training, and privacy decisions."

### 30. "We are too busy to look at this right now."

- Why the owner is saying it: Timing may be bad or priority is low.
- Wrong response: "This will only take 15 minutes."
- Better response: "That makes sense. If missed follow-up is not urgent this week, the right next step may be a short demo video and a follow-up at a better time."

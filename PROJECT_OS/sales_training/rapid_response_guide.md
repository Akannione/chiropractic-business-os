# Rapid Response Guide

Use these concise responses during discovery calls and demos. Keep each answer under 30 seconds.

## Why not Excel?

Excel can work if someone updates it perfectly every day. This system is meant to give the practice a cleaner, demo-ready view of inquiries, follow-ups, weekly activity, and estimated treatment value without rebuilding the same tracker from scratch.

## Why not Google Sheets?

Google Sheets is flexible, but it often turns into a shared list with inconsistent follow-up habits. This gives you a simple practice-focused workflow: enter the inquiry, schedule the follow-up, review what is due, and check the weekly summary.

## Why not HubSpot?

HubSpot is powerful, but it can be more CRM than a small practice needs. This is intentionally lighter: patient inquiries, follow-ups, practice visibility, and exports without a larger sales platform setup.

## Why not my EHR?

Your EHR should stay the source for clinical records. This does not replace it. The goal is to make new inquiry follow-up visible before someone becomes an active patient and to give the owner a simple weekly operating view.

## Why should I pay for this?

You are paying for a simple setup that helps reduce missed inquiry follow-up and gives you clearer weekly visibility. Even recovering one or two missed opportunities can help justify the setup cost, but results vary and are not guaranteed.

## How do I know it will work?

It works when the practice consistently enters inquiries and reviews follow-ups. The system is intentionally simple so staff can use it without a heavy rollout. The first week is about making every inquiry and follow-up visible.

## What if my staff will not use it?

That is a real concern. The setup includes training and a simple workflow so staff only need to enter the inquiry, update status, and set the next follow-up. If it feels too complicated, we simplify the process before go-live.

## What support is included?

Support depends on the package, but the core support is setup guidance, staff training, and monthly review guidance. The goal is to help the practice use the system consistently, not just hand over a tool.

# Top 10 Demo Killers

Use this before every chiropractor demo. The goal is to avoid trust-damaging mistakes and keep the walkthrough focused on business outcomes.

## 1. Talking Too Much About Technology

- Why it hurts trust: The owner wants to understand missed inquiries, follow-ups, and value, not Streamlit, SQLite, or schemas.
- Better approach: Say the tool is a simple practice dashboard, then show the workflow.

## 2. Making Revenue Guarantees

- Why it hurts trust: Guarantees sound inflated and create legal or credibility risk.
- Better approach: Use the ROI calculator as a discussion estimate only. Say results vary by practice and are not guaranteed.

## 3. Attacking Existing Software

- Why it hurts trust: The owner may like their EHR, scheduler, spreadsheet, or CRM.
- Better approach: Respect existing tools and position the Business OS as a narrow inquiry and follow-up view.

## 4. Overselling ROI

- Why it hurts trust: Inflated numbers make the offer feel like a sales trick.
- Better approach: Use conservative inputs and focus on the cost of missed follow-up consistency.

## 5. Pretending It Is Production-Ready For Real Patient Data

- Why it hurts trust: Privacy and compliance questions are serious.
- Better approach: Say the demo uses fake data and production use requires hosting, access, privacy, and compliance planning.

## 6. Showing Every Tab Before Explaining The Problem

- Why it hurts trust: The demo feels like a feature tour instead of a business conversation.
- Better approach: Start with the problem: scattered inquiries and missed follow-ups. Then show the dashboard.

## 7. Ignoring Staff Workflow

- Why it hurts trust: Owners know tools fail if the front desk does not use them.
- Better approach: Explain the smallest routine: add inquiry, set follow-up date, review due and overdue follow-ups.

## 8. Dodging Pricing Questions

- Why it hurts trust: Avoidance makes the offer feel vague.
- Better approach: Explain the package level calmly and tie scope to setup, training, and handoff.

## 9. Using Too Much Jargon

- Why it hurts trust: Terms like schema, KPI, and pipeline can sound detached from practice operations.
- Better approach: Use practice language: patient inquiries, follow-ups, active patients, weekly summary, and estimated treatment value.

## 10. Ending Without A Clear Next Step

- Why it hurts trust: A good demo can stall if the close is vague.
- Better approach: Ask whether the workflow fits, confirm the objection or blocker, and agree on one next action.

## Demo Reminder

Keep the walkthrough simple:

1. Show the missed follow-up problem.
2. Show the Practice Performance Dashboard.
3. Show one patient inquiry update.
4. Show Weekly Summary.
5. Use the ROI calculator conservatively if the owner asks about value.
6. Close with a clear next step.
